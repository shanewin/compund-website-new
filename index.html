Explore Bushwick

Once an industrial area, Bushwick has transformed into one of Brooklyn's most vibrant neighborhoods, known for its incredible street art, diverse food scene, and creative energy. This guide will help you explore the best spots near The Compound.

Is Bushwick a Good Place to Live?

Moving to Bushwick: What You Need to Know

Bushwick has emerged as one of Brooklyn's most desirable neighborhoods for young professionals and creatives. With Bushwick rent prices offering better value than neighboring Williamsburg, it's an ideal choice for those seeking authentic Brooklyn living.

Direct L train access to Manhattan (20 minutes to Union Square)
Thriving arts and music scene with venues like House of Yes
Diverse dining from trendy cafés to authentic Latin cuisine
More affordable than Williamsburg with similar amenities
Growing selection of new construction apartments Bushwick

Bushwick vs Williamsburg: Making the Right Choice

When comparing Bushwick vs Williamsburg, Bushwick offers a more authentic Brooklyn experience with better value. Our North Brooklyn apartments provide the perfect balance of urban convenience and neighborhood charm.

Top Attractions & Landmarks

Bushwick is famous for its incredible street art and cultural spaces. Don't miss these iconic spots:

The Bushwick Collective

Open-air gallery featuring massive murals by world-renowned street artists. This outdoor museum transforms ordinary walls into extraordinary canvases.

15 min walk | Troutman St & St. Nicholas Ave

House of Yes

Immersive performance venue known for spectacular themed parties, circus acts, and boundary-pushing entertainment experiences.

20 min walk | 2 Wyckoff Ave

Knockdown Center

Massive former factory turned contemporary art space and event venue, hosting everything from gallery exhibitions to large-scale performances.

25 min walk | 52-19 Flushing Ave

Food & Dining

From authentic Salvadoran eateries to innovative dining experiences, Bushwick's food scene has something for every palate.

Tulum Brooklyn

Highly-rated casual restaurant serving Mexican cuisine and margaritas, including brunch.

13 min walk | 1056 Broadway

Mao Mao

Red-lit, bi-level Thai eatery with vintage movie posters and projector screenings. Features excellent Thai dishes and their signature Pik Gai Zapp fried chicken wings dusted with homemade sour laab chili powder.

16 min walk | 1000 Broadway

Roberta's

Legendary pizzeria with creative wood-fired pizzas and an iconic vibe. A must-visit destination that put Bushwick on the culinary map.

18 min walk | 261 Moore St

Bars & Nightlife

From cozy cocktail bars to legendary dance clubs, Bushwick offers diverse nightlife options for every mood.

Secret Pour

Cool, under-the-radar bar with a chill, artsy vibe featuring pool tables, arcade games, and a jukebox. Roberto, the passionate bartender-owner, crafts perfect cocktails in this welcoming neighborhood spot with a secret event space in the back.

10 min walk | 114 DeKalb Ave

Ornithology Jazz Club

An incredible jazz experience featuring two distinct locations just steps apart. Start with intimate conversation over wine and relaxed jazz, then move to the swankier main venue for unbelievable live performances. Perfect for date nights and music lovers.

16 min walk | 6 Suydam St

The Tiny Cupboard Comedy Club

A standout indie comedy venue featuring diverse performances in intimate indoor spaces and a unique rooftop setting. With affordable shows ($5-$10) and 2-5 daily performances showcasing both emerging and established talent, it's a cultural hotspot capturing Brooklyn's vibrant arts scene.

19 min walk | 10 Cooper St

Parks & Recreation

Bushwick offers several green spaces for relaxation, sports, and community activities.

Maria Hernandez Park

The heart of Bushwick, featuring basketball courts, playgrounds, and frequent community events. A true neighborhood gathering place.

19 min walk | Knickerbocker Ave & Starr St

Irving Square Park

Bushwick's town square spanning an entire city block, popular with picnickers, dog owners, and families. Features a children's playground near Knickerbocker Avenue with springs, spray shower, and colorful climbing structure.

20 min walk | Knickerbocker Ave & Weirfield St

Bushwick City Farm

Community garden and urban farm offering fresh produce and educational programs. Supporting local sustainability efforts.

18 min walk | 354 Stockton St
